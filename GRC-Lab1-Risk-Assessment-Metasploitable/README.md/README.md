## 🎯 Executive Summary

Performed end-to-end Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) assessment on a simulated vulnerable environment (Metasploitable 2).  
Translated raw technical scans (Nmap, Nikto, Nuclei) into **quantitative business risk** with a total **Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE) of $1.28 million USD**.

Key risks included vsFTPd backdoor, weak credentials, and outdated services. All findings mapped to **CIS Controls v8**, **NIST CSF 2.0**, and **NIST SP 800-53**. Delivered clear executive recommendations suitable for real client reporting.

This artifact demonstrates core GRC skills: attack surface analysis, risk quantification, framework alignment, and risk treatment planning.

## 🔍 Key Risk Findings

| Risk | ALE (USD) | Severity | Recommended Treatment |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------------------|
| vsFTPd Backdoor (CVE-2011-2523) | $640,000 | Critical | Mitigate immediately |
| Weak/Default Credentials | $486,000 | High | Mitigate |
| Unencrypted + Outdated Services | $790,000+ | High | Mitigate |

**Total ALE: $1.28 million USD**

## ✅ Risk Treatment Plan
1. **Mitigate** high/critical risks (patch, disable unnecessary services, enforce strong authentication)
2. **Transfer** residual risk via cyber insurance where appropriate
3. Implement continuous monitoring and secure configuration baselines
